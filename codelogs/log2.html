CSS Measuring Units

Absoulte vs. Relative

CSS length units fall in two categories: absolute and relavite

Absolute units are fixed measurements that do not change based on other factors like viewport size or screen size. Examples include px (pixels),pt (points), in (inches) and cm(centimeters). These units are widely used for we designing.

Examples of Measuirng Units

1. px and pt

A pixel often represents a tiny dot on a screen.

A point is a traditional print unit. They are rarely used for responsive design.

2. em and rem (Relative Units)

Em: Relative to the font-size of the parent element. Multiple em values can actually become confusing.

Rem: Relative to the root element's font-size.

3. VW and VH (Viewport Units)

VW: Relative to the width of the viewport.

VH: Relative to the height of the viewport.

These units are useful for full-screen layouts and scaling when windows are resized.
